Give a warm weclome to Mars!



Named after the Roman god of war, Mars is the fourth planet in the solar system; and is also the last of the terrestrial planets. The landmass between Mars and Earth is very similar. Ever see the movie John Carter? In that movie he ends up on Mars, and one of the first things he realizes is the gravity is off. This is because the gravity on Mars is 37% of the gravity on Earth. This means you could jump three times higher than on Earth. Mars also experiences huge dust storms that can last up to a month long. This is due to the planets orbit pattern; instead of having a circilar orbit, the planet has more of an elliptical shape.


As all readers are aware there is life on Earth, but did you know that Mars is the most hospitable to life? They tried this in the movie The Martian, which had a lot of potential... poor Matt Damon. Mars also have longer years than Earth. 687 days to orbit the sun. Mars also contains ice, and you know what that means! Water! Besides Earth, Mars is the only planet that has polar ice caps. Mars also experiences seasons however they are twice as long as Earthm, and this is because of the tilt of the planets axis. Earth has a tilt of 22.5 degrees while Mars has a tilt of 25.19 degrees. The two moons of Mars, Phobos and Deimos were written in the book Gullivers Travels by Jonathan Swift, the moons were discovered 151 years later.
