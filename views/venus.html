Greetings Venus!




The morning and evening star! In early times people believed that Venus was two stars, being Venus overtakes earths orbit due to having shorter orbit times as Earth, the planet would shift from only being seen in the morning to only being seen at night. Similar to that of Mercury, Venus has a longer day than earth. 243 Earth days to complete one rotation of the planet and 225 Earth days for one full year. Not as fast as Mercury but dang that is still quick. Venus is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty due to the brightness of the planet. Babylonians in 1581 referred to Venus as "bright queen of the sky".

Most other planets rotate counter-clockwise on their axis, however Venus rotates clockwise. This is noteable called a retrograde rotation and may have been caused by a collision with an asteroid or other object. The atmospheric pressure of Venus is 92 times stronger than Earth's! Any asteroids that enter get crushed by the immense pressure. This pressure when compared to Earth's is similar to being 621.371 miles underneath the ocean. Due to the high atmospheric pressure there are hardly any surface craters on the planet. Even though it is the second planet in the solar system it is the hottest with temperatures reaching to 863℉!
